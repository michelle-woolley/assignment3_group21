Group processes

Our effectiveness in assignment 2 was quite good; however, there were some notable improvements needed. Our communication efficiency wasn’t that strong and our collaboration suffered because of it. There would be times where we were very quick to assist any of our group members' queries; however, there were also periods when there would be little to no communication and it was difficult to not double up on content being written as well as queries not being answered. A major change we hope to employ during assignment 3 is our communication skills. Something as simple as ensuring we are all timely for group meetings and quick to respond to group members queries will go a long way towards easing the stress of putting the assignment on pause because we’re not sure who has attempted what section yet and if anyone is falling behind in their share of the workload.

Another change we will introduce is solid timeframes, as simple as it sounds it was not something that we strictly followed in assignment 2. Things as simple as setting deadlines for certain sections of the assignment would allow us to assess whether anyone is getting stuck on a certain section or even if someone is not managing their time properly. It will also allow us to improve the standard of our work by tackling questions in an open-minded manner as opposed to cramming content and feeling rushed. Rushing the work leaves room for errors and doesn’t allow the group-member to fully apply all their knowledge to the question at hand.

Proofreading was also an important factor for us and it's not that we necessarily didn’t do enough in assignment 2 but there Is always room for improvement. With large assignments such as assignment 2 and 3 it takes a while to proofread all of the content so, instead, we will incrementally proofread our assignment in blocks after each section is completed as opposed to proofreading the entire assignment when it is finalised. This both simplifies the proofreading process as well as improves its effectiveness.

All-in-all, our group worked well together in assignment 2; however, these few changes can make all the difference between us working well and us actually working well together to produce a top-tier assignment. Communication is very important and either makes or breaks a group assignment so I think once we’ve mastered our communication skills and deadline enforcement, we’ll be a very proficient team.

career plans

The groups career plans are very similar in respect to the direction they intend to take yet differ greatly when it comes to their ideal job. Every member in the group aims to progress through RMIT University and achieve a wealth of knowledge by doing so in order to set themselves up for a promising career in the IT sector. Following this, and in some cases during this, they intend to seek external supplementary education in the form of either an internship in their preferred career path or as a paid employee. This is where the similarities end though, only Zorren and Lachlan intend to take similar career paths specifically in the Software Engineering sector. Common elements their career paths hold are things such as team building, software proficiency, personal growth in a career as well as learning to work in a highly competitive environment.

Luke’s career path and plan takes a different approach, with the intent to become a Strategic and International Policy officer. Rather than scouting for an internship, he will enroll in government-based trainee programs that will coincide with his university studies. Despite this his initial growth and knowledge base will still be RMIT among other external readings. Like both Zorren and Lachlan, Luke’s career plan has not changed between assignment 1 and now; maintaining his passion and drive for a career in Strategic International Policies.

Michelle’s plan to pursue a career in web development has not waivered either, in fact, so much so that she is still set on pursuing Frontend Web Development specifically. If anything, her drive for this career in particular has only strengthened. Much like Michelle, Timothy’s career plan has not changed either. He is set on excelling as a Quality Assurance Specialist, specifically in the gaming sector. This is quite fitting as his attitude towards our assessments was meticulous. He was sure to speak out to ensure everyone was up to speed during meetings and general communications as well as reaching out privately to ensure everyone was doing well and nothing was stunting their progress. Jorin’s preference was in a similar sector to Timothy’s, they both plan to pursue careers in the gaming sector however Jorin plans to specialize as a Senior Developer. His passion for this career was evident from the beginning much like the other group members so it came to no surprise that when asked the question of whether he would change his career decision he was quick to say no.

Everything considered, none of our group members decided to change their career plans in response to their feedback from assignment 1 or 2 so it's difficult to discuss if there were common elements between their old career plan and thus their lack thereof a new career plan. However, if we were to analyze the common elements between all of our career plans it would be that time management, group cohesion and punctuality are all major factors that we share. Additionally, the ability to work in a high stress environment as well as having the ambition to continue the learning process as our career plans progress are also factors our careers would share, interestingly enough, these are also all factors that needed to be employed in producing our group assignments in order to achieve success. Assessing the similarities and differences of said careers, it would be true to say that all specialized industries actually collide, for instance Zorren and Lachlan's choice of Software Engineering in many instances is tightly integrated with Michelle’s career choice of Frontend Web Development. A use case would be in an instance where software is being developed by the engineers and Michelle would have to work closely with them in order to assess themes and content that the software contains that would be ideal for marketing and displaying on the web side of the business. Even more so if the software happened to be game related then both Jorin and Timothy’s career paths would also be involved in the process so it's quite evident that these careers integrate frequently. Luke’s career plan however is a specialty profession where it is intentionally not harmonious with other similar IT careers due to the fact that sensitive national data is the underlying nature of the career.

Our career plans nonetheless are almost identical where we intend to pursue university level studies in conjunction with either an internship or paid job placement at a relevant firm and grow both our knowledge and career progress throughout said employment.
